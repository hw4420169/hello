Selecting the ideal running shoe depends on various factors, including your mileage, foot shape, and the type of runs you enjoy. As of May 2025, consumer conversations highlight a mix of comfort, tech-forward cushioning, and sustainable materials. We’ve compared top shoes to help you find the best pairing for your needs.

Brooks Ghost 17

A versatile daily trainer known for its comfort and efficiency, suitable for both tempo runs and recovery sessions.

Nike Alphafly 3

A racing day hero; this lightweight shoe features ZoomX cushioning and a carbon plate, engineered to help you reach podium pace.

Hoka Clifton 9

Ideal for long-distance runs, the Clifton 9 provides ample cushioning without sacrificing responsiveness, ensuring comfort over marathon training blocks.

Puma Deviate Nitro 3

Balances cushioning and stability, making it suitable for both daily training and speed workouts.

Asics Novablast 4

Known for its bouncy and energetic feel, this shoe is great for runners seeking a fun ride on tempo sessions.

When choosing a running shoe, consider factors such as cushioning, stability, weight, and the specific demands of your training block. It’s important to try on shoes in person when possible, ensuring they meet your comfort and performance needs.
